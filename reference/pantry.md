# Pantry and default ingredient assumptions

## Purpose
When a recipe does not specify an ingredient variant, use these defaults.

## Rice
- Sona masoori: preferred for everyday South Indian rice meals, bisi bele bath, curd rice
- Basmati: preferred for pulao, biryani-style rice, North Indian rice dishes

## Dals and pulses
- Toor dal
- Moong dal
- Chana dal
- Masoor dal
- Urad dal
- Rajma
- Chickpeas

## Oils and fats
- Ghee: preferred finishing fat for many Indian dishes
- Mustard oil: used where regionally authentic, especially certain fish or North Indian dishes when appropriate
- Neutral oil: sunflower / vegetable for general cooking
- Olive oil: for Mediterranean and Western dishes
- Coconut oil: for Kerala-style dishes when relevant
- Butter: for makhani dishes and some finishing

## Spice powders
- Turmeric
- Kashmiri chilli powder
- Hot chilli powder
- Coriander powder
- Cumin powder
- Garam masala
- Amchur
- Kasoori methi
- Black pepper

## Whole spices
- Cumin seeds
- Mustard seeds
- Coriander seeds
- Fennel seeds
- Fenugreek seeds
- Cloves
- Green cardamom
- Cinnamon
- Bay leaf
- Dry red chillies

## Condiments
- Soy sauce
- Vinegar
- MSG
- Tamarind
- Lemon juice
- Yogurt / curd
- Tahini
- Miso
- Peanut butter

## Fresh staples
- Onion
- Tomato
- Ginger
- Garlic
- Green chilli
- Coriander
- Mint
- Curry leaves

## Notes
- Prefer authenticity over convenience where practical.
- Use UK ingredient names where possible.
- If a recipe depends strongly on a particular ingredient variant, specify it explicitly.
