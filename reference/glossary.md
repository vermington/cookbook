## UK kitchen vocabulary
- courgette, not zucchini
- aubergine, not eggplant
- coriander, not cilantro
- grill for UK oven top-heat context, but "broil" may be noted parenthetically if helpful
- tin where natural in UK context, but tray/dish is often clearer

## Indian cooking terms
- bhuna: cook down the masala until thick, glossy, and aromatic
- tadka / tempering: hot fat infused with spices and added to a dish
- hung curd: yogurt strained until thick
- curry cut: butcher-cut bone-in meat pieces suitable for curry
- gravy: sauce component of an Indian dish, not British brown gravy unless specified
- tari: fat or oil layer on top of a finished curry

## Style notes
- Preserve culturally specific words when they add clarity.
- Do not over-translate well-known Indian terms into awkward English.
