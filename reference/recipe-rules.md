# Recipe Rules

## Purpose
Ensure all recipes are consistent, usable, and high quality.

---

## Structure Rules
Every recipe must include:
- Summary
- Ingredients (grouped logically)
- Method (numbered steps)
- Notes
- Serving suggestions

Optional:
- Variants
- Personal notes
- Make-ahead instructions

---

## Ingredient Rules
- Use metric units where appropriate
- Use tsp/tbsp where natural
- Avoid vague quantities (“some oil”)
- Group ingredients by usage (marinade, base, finish)

---

## Method Rules
- Use numbered steps
- Keep each step concise
- Include key cues:
  - colour
  - texture
  - aroma
- Avoid vague instructions

---

## Consistency Rules
- No unnecessary repetition
- Avoid overlong introductions
- Keep formatting uniform

---

## Cooking Logic Rules
- Do not skip essential steps (bhuna, resting, tempering)
- Do not add unnecessary steps
- Prefer fewer, meaningful actions

---

## Adaptation Rules
- Always consider:
  - equipment available
  - ingredient availability
- Suggest substitutions only when safe

---

## Error Handling
If uncertain:
- Add a short note instead of guessing
- Flag ambiguity with a TODO

---

## Personal Notes Handling
- Preserve user notes exactly
- Never overwrite
- Append new notes clearly
