# House Preferences

## Core Philosophy
- Authenticity first.
- Home-style over restaurant-style unless explicitly requested.
- Recipes should be practical, repeatable, and realistic in a UK home kitchen.
- Avoid unnecessary richness (excess cream, butter, or tomato).
- Prefer balanced flavours over extreme spice or sweetness.

---

## Indian Cooking Preferences

### Regional Priorities
- North Indian: UP Kayastha home-style preferred.
- South Indian: Bangalore / Karnataka flavour preferred.
- Indo-Chinese: North Eastern hills flavour preferred (clean, not overly saucy).
- Regional dishes: must reflect their authentic origin.

---

### Flavour Guidelines
- Avoid overly sweet gravies (especially in makhani dishes).
- Tomato should not dominate unless dish demands it.
- Use kasoori methi thoughtfully — never excessive.
- Use ghee for finishing where appropriate.
- Mustard oil only where regionally appropriate.

---

### Texture Expectations
- Curries: thick but flowing, not watery.
- Dry sabzis: glossy, not wet.
- Rice dishes: grains separate, not mushy.
- Fried rice: dry, high-heat, not greasy.
- Paneer: soft, not rubbery.

---

## Cooking Style Preferences
- Prefer fewer dishes cooked well over large spreads.
- Prefer one-pot or efficient workflows where possible.
- Recipes should include timing guidance when helpful.
- Avoid unnecessary steps unless they materially improve outcome.

---

## Ingredient Preferences
- Prefer fresh aromatics (ginger, garlic, green chilli).
- Use pantry staples intelligently (not over-complicated recipes).
- UK ingredient names should be used by default.

---

## Recipe Writing Preferences
- Clear, structured, minimal fluff.
- Include key cooking cues (colour, texture, smell).
- Include “why” only when it improves execution.
- Avoid overlong ingredient lists unless necessary.

---

## Personal Workflow Notes
- User often cooks:
  - weeknight quick meals
  - weekend slightly elevated dishes
- Frequently uses:
  - AEG oven
  - slow cooker
  - stovetop
- Prefers:
  - adaptable recipes
  - multi-use components (gravy bases, marinades)

---

## Absolute Do Nots
- Do not invent “restaurant-style” versions unless asked.
- Do not overload dishes with cream or butter.
- Do not write vague instructions (“cook till done”).
- Do not ignore equipment constraints.
