# Mushroom Pea Risotto

## Summary
Mushroom-led risotto with chestnut mushrooms, peas, parmesan, and parsley.  
The peas add sweetness and colour, but the dish should still taste primarily of browned mushrooms and stock.

---

## Ingredients

### Main
- Arborio rice - 320 g
- Chestnut mushrooms - 500-600 g, sliced or torn
- Frozen peas - 150 g
- Knorr chicken stock pot - 1
- Hot water - 1.2-1.4 litres
- Parmesan - 70-90 g, finely grated
- Parsley - small handful, finely chopped
- Banana shallots - 2 large, finely chopped
- Garlic - 3 cloves, finely chopped
- Butter - 50 g, divided
- Olive oil - 1 tbsp
- White wine - 150 ml, optional
- Black pepper - to taste
- Salt - to taste

### Optional
- Thyme - 1 tsp fresh leaves or 1/2 tsp dried
- Lemon juice - small squeeze, only if needed
- Onion - 1 medium, finely chopped, if banana shallots are unavailable

---

## Method

### 1. Make stock
- Dissolve the chicken stock pot in 1.2 litres hot water.
- Keep the stock hot in a saucepan or jug.
- Taste before adding salt later; stock pot and parmesan both bring salt.

---

### 2. Brown mushrooms
- Heat a wide pan on medium-high.
- Add olive oil and 15 g butter.
- Add mushrooms with a small pinch of salt.
- Cook 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they release water, the water evaporates, and the mushrooms brown.
- Remove about half the mushrooms to use as a topping.

**Cue:** mushrooms should be browned and savoury, not grey and wet.

---

### 3. Start base
- Lower heat to medium.
- Add 15 g butter.
- Add banana shallots and cook 4-5 minutes until soft and translucent, not browned.
- Add garlic and thyme if using.
- Cook 30 seconds.

---

### 4. Toast rice
- Add arborio rice.
- Stir for 1-2 minutes until the grains look glossy and the edges turn slightly translucent.

---

### 5. Add wine
- Add white wine if using.
- Stir until mostly absorbed and the alcohol smell fades.

---

### 6. Cook risotto
- Add hot stock one ladle at a time.
- Stir often, not constantly.
- Add the next ladle when the previous one is mostly absorbed.
- Cook 18-22 minutes total, until the rice is creamy but still has a slight bite.
- Add extra hot water if the rice needs more time after the stock is used.

---

### 7. Add peas
- Add frozen peas for the final 3-4 minutes of cooking.
- Stir until peas are hot but still bright.

---

### 8. Finish
- Turn heat off.
- Add remaining 20 g butter and most of the parmesan.
- Cover and rest 2 minutes.
- Stir vigorously until glossy and creamy.
- Adjust with a splash of hot stock or water if too thick.

---

### 9. Serve
- Top with reserved mushrooms, remaining parmesan, parsley, and black pepper.
- Add a small squeeze of lemon only if the risotto tastes flat.

---

## Notes
- Babycorn is not recommended in this risotto; it pushes the dish toward stir-fry texture and flavour.
- Final texture should be loose and flowing, not stiff or mound-like.
- Brown the mushrooms properly before starting the rice; this is the main flavour base.
- Banana shallots suit the dish better than onion because they are softer and less assertive.
- Salt only at the end if needed.

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Serve immediately.
- Works with a sharp green salad.
- Keep portions modest if serving with chicken or fish.

---

## Variants
- Vegetarian version: use vegetable stock instead of chicken stock.
- More mushroom-forward version: add 10 g dried porcini, soaked, and use the strained soaking liquid as part of the stock.
