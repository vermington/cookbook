# Pea Risotto with Mushrooms

## Summary
Pea-forward risotto for when there are not enough mushrooms for a mushroom-led version.  
Small amounts of browned chestnut mushroom, miso, soy sauce, and parmesan rind add depth without turning the dish into a stir-fry.

---

## Ingredients

### Main
- Arborio rice - 320 g
- Frozen peas - 250 g
- Chestnut mushrooms - 150 g, thinly sliced
- Knorr chicken stock pot - 1
- Hot water - 1.2 litres, plus extra if needed
- Miso - 1 tsp
- Soy sauce - 1/2 tsp
- Parmesan rind - 1 piece, optional but useful
- Parmesan - 90 g, finely grated
- Parsley - small handful, finely chopped
- Banana shallots - 2 large, finely chopped
- Garlic - 3 cloves, finely chopped
- Butter - 50 g, divided
- Olive oil - 1 tbsp
- White wine - 150 ml, optional
- Black pepper - to taste
- Salt - only if needed

### Optional
- Lemon juice - a few drops, only if the risotto tastes flat
- Onion - 1 medium, finely chopped, if banana shallots are unavailable

---

## Method

### 1. Make stock
- Dissolve the chicken stock pot in 1.2 litres hot water.
- Add parmesan rind, if using, and keep the stock hot.
- Whisk in miso and soy sauce.
- Taste the stock before adding salt later.

**Cue:** stock should taste savoury but not aggressively salty. Dilute with 100-200 ml hot water if needed.

---

### 2. Brown mushrooms
- Heat a wide pan on medium-high.
- Add olive oil and 15 g butter.
- Add the sliced mushrooms.
- Cook 6-8 minutes until their water evaporates and the edges darken.
- Remove most of the mushrooms for topping.

**Cue:** mushrooms should be concentrated and browned; with only 150 g, do not leave them grey or wet.

---

### 3. Start base
- Lower heat to medium.
- Add 15 g butter.
- Add banana shallots and cook 4-5 minutes until soft and translucent, not browned.
- Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.

---

### 4. Toast rice
- Add arborio rice.
- Stir 1-2 minutes until glossy and slightly translucent at the edges.

---

### 5. Add wine
- Add white wine if using.
- Stir until mostly absorbed and the alcohol smell fades.

---

### 6. Cook risotto
- Add hot stock one ladle at a time.
- Stir often, not constantly.
- Add the next ladle when the previous one is mostly absorbed.
- Cook 18-22 minutes total, until the rice is creamy but still has a slight bite.
- Add extra hot water if the rice needs more time after the stock is used.

---

### 7. Add peas
- Add frozen peas for the final 3-4 minutes of cooking.
- Stir until the peas are hot but still bright.

---

### 8. Finish
- Turn heat off.
- Remove parmesan rind.
- Add remaining 20 g butter and most of the parmesan.
- Cover and rest 2 minutes.
- Stir vigorously until glossy and creamy.
- Adjust with a splash of hot stock or water if too thick.

---

### 9. Serve
- Top with reserved mushrooms, remaining parmesan, parsley, and black pepper.
- Add a few drops of lemon juice only if it tastes flat.

---

## Notes
- This is not a classic mushroom risotto; peas carry the dish and mushrooms act as an accent.
- Keep soy sauce to 1/2 tsp. More will push the flavour away from risotto.
- Miso and parmesan rind compensate for the low mushroom quantity.
- Babycorn is not recommended in the risotto; serve it separately if needed.
- Salt only at the end, after tasting.

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Serve immediately.
- Works with a sharp green salad.
- Can take a simple grilled chicken or fish alongside if needed.

---

## Variants
- Vegetarian version: use vegetable stock instead of chicken stock.
- More mushroom-forward version: use [[mushroom-pea-risotto]] when 500-600 g mushrooms are available.
