# Fish Curry

## Summary
Light coconut-based Kerala-style fish curry with tamarind.  
Clean, slightly tangy, and designed to be eaten with rice.

---

## Ingredients

- White fish (cod / haddock) – 700–750 g, cut into large pieces
- Onion – 1 large, sliced
- Garlic – 4 cloves, minced
- Ginger – 1 inch, minced
- Green chillies – 2, slit
- Curry leaves – 10–12
- Coconut oil – 2 tbsp

- Coconut milk – 600 ml
- Tamarind extract – 1½ tbsp
- Turmeric – ½ tsp
- Red chilli powder – 1 tsp
- Coriander powder – 2 tsp
- Salt – to taste
- Water – ~¾ cup

---

## Method

### 1. Build base
- Heat coconut oil
- Add onion, sauté until soft (not browned)
- Add garlic, ginger, chilli, curry leaves → cook until aromatic

---

### 2. Add spices
- Add turmeric, chilli powder, coriander powder
- Stir with a splash of water to prevent burning

---

### 3. Form gravy
- Add tamarind extract
- Add coconut milk + water
- Simmer gently 5 minutes

---

### 4. Cook fish
- Add fish pieces carefully
- Simmer 8–10 minutes on low

**Do not stir aggressively — swirl pan instead**

---

### 5. Finish
- Taste and adjust salt/tamarind
- Optional: drizzle 1 tsp coconut oil

---

## Notes

- Fish should just flake — overcooking breaks it
- Coconut milk must not boil hard → risk splitting
- Tamarind should be present but not dominant

---

## Serving Suggestions
- With sona masoori rice

---

## Guest Upgrade

- Add a few pearl onions instead of sliced onion
- Finish with fresh coriander
- Add a small piece of kodampuli if available (authentic souring)
