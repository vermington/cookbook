# Chicken Kathi Roll

## Summary
Kolkata-style chicken kathi rolls with oven-grilled hung-curd tikka filling, onion salad, green chutney, and egg-coated parathas.  
Designed for guests: the chicken cooks in the oven while the rolls are assembled fresh on the tawa.

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken filling
- [[chicken|Boneless chicken thigh and/or breast]] - 900 g
- Hung curd or thick [[yogurt]] - 5 1/2 tbsp
- [[ginger|Ginger]]-[[garlic]] paste - 1 1/2-2 tbsp
- Kashmiri chilli powder or degi mirch - 2 tsp
- Coriander powder - 2 tsp
- Cumin powder - 1 tsp
- Garam masala - 1 tsp
- Turmeric - 1/2 tsp
- Kasoori methi - 1 tsp, crushed
- Mustard oil - 1 1/2 tbsp
- Lemon juice - 1 tbsp
- Salt - 1 1/4 tsp

### Rolls
- Parathas - 6-8
- Eggs - 6-8
- Oil or ghee - for cooking

### Onion salad
- [[onion|Onions]] - 2, thinly sliced
- Lemon juice - to taste
- Chaat masala - to taste
- Salt - small pinch

### Green chutney
- Coriander leaves and tender stems - 1 packed cup
- Mint leaves - 1/2 packed cup
- Green chillies - 2
- Ginger - 1/2 inch
- Lemon juice - 1 1/2 tbsp
- Roasted cumin powder - 1/2 tsp, optional
- Chaat masala - 1/2 tsp, optional
- Salt - 1/2 tsp, adjust
- Sugar - 1/4 tsp, optional
- Water - 1-2 tbsp, only as needed

---

## Method

### 1. Cut chicken
- Cut thigh into 2-3 cm chunks or short strips.
- Cut breast slightly larger or thicker than thigh so it does not dry out.
- Keep thigh and breast on different areas of the tray if using both.

**Cue:** pieces should be roll-sized, not large tikka chunks.

---

### 2. Marinate chicken
- Mix hung curd, ginger-garlic paste, spices, kasoori methi, mustard oil, lemon juice, and salt.
- Add chicken and coat well.
- Rest at least 30 minutes; longer is better if time allows.

**Cue:** marinade should cling to the chicken and not pool.

---

### 3. Oven-grill chicken
- Heat the AEG oven to 220°C fan + grill mode.
- Spread chicken on a lined tray in a single layer.
- Place the tray on the second rack from the top.
- Cook for 10 minutes.
- Move to the top rack and cook 4-6 minutes more for char.
- Take the tray out of the oven and rest 5 minutes outside the oven.

**Cue:** chicken should be juicy but not wet, with light char and masala clinging to the pieces.

---

### 4. Tighten filling if needed
- If there is watery liquid on the tray, scrape chicken and masala into a hot pan.
- Cook on high for 2-3 minutes until the masala clings.

---

### 5. Make onion salad
- Mix sliced onions with lemon juice, salt, and chaat masala.
- Rest 10 minutes.

---

### 6. Make chutney
- Blend coriander, mint, chillies, ginger, lemon juice, cumin, chaat masala, salt, sugar, and minimal water.
- Taste and adjust lemon and salt.

**Cue:** chutney should be sharp, fresh, and spreadable, not watery.

---

### 7. Cook egg paratha
- Heat tawa on medium.
- Warm one paratha on one side.
- Beat one egg with a pinch of salt.
- Pour the egg on the tawa and place the paratha over it.
- Press lightly until the egg bonds, then flip briefly.

---

### 8. Assemble
- Place paratha egg-side inside.
- Spread green chutney.
- Add chicken filling.
- Add onion salad.
- Roll tightly and wrap in foil or paper.

---

## Notes
- One marinade is better here than two; chutney, onion, egg, and paratha already bring enough variation.
- Tray-grilling is better than skewers for kathi roll filling because it is faster and keeps the smaller pieces juicier.
- If using mixed thigh and breast, check breast pieces first because they dry faster.
- Rest chicken outside the oven, not inside, so residual heat does not overcook it.

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Serve hot as dinner.
- Add extra lemon wedges, chilli sauce, or masala nimbu soda.

---

## Variants
- Boneless thigh-only version: use the same marinade for 900 g thigh and cook as written.
- Spicier version: add 1/2 tsp hot chilli powder to the marinade.
- No-egg version: cook parathas normally and assemble with extra chutney.

---

## Personal Notes
- Worked quite nicely for dinner with guests.
- Hung curd was already prepared in the morning and worked well for the chicken marinade.
- Oven method was easier for multitasking than pan-cooking the filling.
- Rest after the top-rack grill step should be outside the oven.
