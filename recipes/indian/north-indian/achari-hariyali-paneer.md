# Achari Hariyali Paneer

## Summary
Quick dry paneer sabzi using dense leftover green chutney as the fresh base and achari masala as a background accent.  
The final texture is coating-style: glossy, punchy, and not a gravy.

---

## Ingredients

### Main
- [[paneer]] - 200-250 g, cubed
- Leftover green chutney - 2 tbsp
- [[onion|Onion]] - 1 medium, sliced or chopped
- Mustard oil - 1 tbsp
- Ghee or neutral oil - 1 tsp, optional
- Cumin seeds - 1/2 tsp
- Achari masala - 1/2-3/4 tsp
- Turmeric - tiny pinch, optional
- Kashmiri chilli powder - 1/4 tsp, optional
- Amchur - tiny pinch, only if needed
- Kasoori methi - pinch, optional
- Salt - only after tasting

---

## Method

### 1. Start oil and onion
- Heat mustard oil on medium-high until just smoking.
- Lower to medium.
- Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
- Add onion and cook 6-8 minutes until soft and lightly golden.

---

### 2. Bloom achari masala
- Lower heat to medium-low.
- Add achari masala, turmeric, and chilli powder with 1 tbsp water.
- Cook 30 seconds until aromatic.

---

### 3. Add chutney
- Add green chutney and 1-2 tbsp water.
- Cook 1-2 minutes until glossy and no longer raw-smelling.

**Cue:** chutney should darken slightly but still taste fresh.

---

### 4. Finish paneer
- Add paneer and toss gently.
- Cover on low for 2 minutes, just to heat through.
- Finish with kasoori methi and optional ghee.
- Taste before adding salt or amchur.

---

## Notes
- Keep achari masala under 1 tsp; it should sit behind the coriander-mint chutney, not dominate.
- Leftover chutney may already be salty and sour, so add salt and amchur only at the end.
- Do not simmer into a gravy or the coriander and mint can turn dull.

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Roti
- Paratha
- Quick roll with sliced onions

---

## Variants
- Bhurji version: crumble the paneer instead of cubing it and cook 1 minute less at the end.

---

## Personal Notes
- Made with leftover dense green chutney from chicken kathi rolls.
- Worked nicely as a quick paneer dish.
