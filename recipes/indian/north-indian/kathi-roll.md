# Paneer Kathi Roll

## Summary
Kolkata-style paneer kathi roll with egg-coated paratha and tandoori-style paneer filling.  
The key is dry, well-coated paneer and a properly bonded egg layer.

---

## Ingredients

### Paneer filling
- [[paneer]] – 250 g, cut into strips or cubes
- [[yogurt|Thick yogurt]] – 3 tbsp
- Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tsp
- Kashmiri chilli powder – 1 tsp
- Turmeric – ¼ tsp
- Coriander powder – 1 tsp
- Garam masala – ½ tsp
- Kasoori methi – 1 tsp, crushed
- Mustard oil – 1 tbsp
- Salt – to taste
- Lemon juice – 1 tsp

---

### Rolls
- Parathas – 4
- Eggs – 4 (1 per roll)
- Oil or ghee – for cooking

---

### Assembly
- [[onion|Onion]] – 1 large, thinly sliced
- Green chutney – 2–3 tbsp
- Chaat masala – ½ tsp
- Lemon juice – to taste

---

## Method

### 1. Marinate paneer
- Mix all marinade ingredients
- Coat paneer well
- Rest 20–30 minutes (longer if possible)

---

### 2. Cook paneer (oven method)

- Preheat oven to **220°C (fan + grill mode)**
- Spread paneer in single layer on tray
- Lightly brush with oil

Cook:
- **8–10 minutes total**
- Turn once midway

**Critical cue:**
- Edges slightly charred
- Surface dry but interior soft

---

### Alternative: Hob method (recommended for control)
- Heat pan on high
- Add small amount of oil
- Cook paneer undisturbed for 1–2 minutes before turning
- Cook until lightly charred and masala clings

---

### 3. Cook egg paratha
- Heat tawa
- Place paratha, cook one side
- Flip and crack 1 egg directly on surface
- Spread egg evenly
- Flip again so egg cooks onto paratha
- Cook until set and lightly golden

---

### 4. Assemble
- Place paratha (egg side inside)
- Spread chutney
- Add paneer filling
- Add onions
- Sprinkle chaat masala
- Add lemon juice

---

### 5. Roll
- Fold bottom slightly
- Roll tightly
- Wrap in foil or paper

---

## Notes

### Paneer cooking (critical)
- Overcooking = rubbery paneer
- Oven dries faster than expected
- Prefer slightly undercooked → finishes in roll

### Moisture control
- Filling must be dry
- No visible liquid

### Egg layer
- Should be thin and bonded to paratha, not scrambled

---

## Personal Notes
- At 220°C fan + grill, 12 minutes overcooks paneer
- 10 minutes max, with frequent checks, gives best texture
- Oven works, but hob gives better control for small batches

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Serve hot
- Works standalone, no accompaniment needed

---

## Guest Upgrade
- Toss onions in salt + vinegar before adding
- Add light drizzle of chilli sauce
- Finish with a pinch of black salt
