# Kadhai Paneer

## Summary
Semi-dry kadhai paneer for 225 g paneer, with peppers, onion, coarse kadhai masala, and a peeled tomato base.  
The final texture should be glossy and coating-style, not a loose gravy.

---

## Ingredients

### Main
- [[paneer]] - 225 g, cut into 2.5-3 cm cubes
- Capsicum or peppers - 1 large or 2 small, cut into chunks
- [[onion|Onion]] - 1 medium, cut into petals or thick slices
- [[tomato|Tomato]] - 1 medium, blanched, peeled, and blended
- Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tbsp
- Green chillies - 1-2, slit
- Ghee - 1 tbsp
- Mustard oil - 1 tbsp
- Salt - to taste

### Kadhai spice
- Coriander seeds - 1 1/2 tsp
- Cumin seeds - 1/2 tsp
- Fennel seeds - 1/2 tsp
- Dry red chillies - 1-2
- Black peppercorns - 4-5

### Powder spices
- Turmeric - 1/4 tsp
- Kashmiri chilli powder - 1 tsp
- Coriander powder - 1/2 tsp, optional only if tomato is watery
- Garam masala - 1/4 tsp
- Kasoori methi - 1/2 tsp, crushed

---

## Method

### 1. Prepare kadhai spice
- Dry roast coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fennel seeds, dry red chillies, and peppercorns on medium-low for 1-2 minutes until aromatic.
- Cool briefly and crush coarsely.

**Cue:** the spice should be gritty, not a fine powder.

---

### 2. Prepare tomato
- Blanch the tomato until the skin loosens.
- Peel and blend to a smooth puree.

---

### 3. Sear paneer
- Heat ghee in a pan on medium-high.
- Lightly sear paneer on 1-2 sides until pale golden.
- Remove to a plate.

---

### 4. Cook onion and peppers
- In the same ghee, add onion and peppers.
- Cook on high for about 4 minutes, tossing regularly.
- Remove to the same plate as the paneer.

**Cue:** onion and peppers should be lightly blistered and no longer raw, but still have bite.

---

### 5. Build masala
- Lower heat to medium.
- Add mustard oil, ginger-garlic paste, and green chillies.
- Cook 30-45 seconds until the raw smell goes.
- Add blended tomato, salt, turmeric, and Kashmiri chilli powder.
- Cook 5-7 minutes until the tomato thickens and the masala turns glossy.

---

### 6. Add kadhai spice
- Add crushed kadhai spice and a splash of water.
- Cook 1 minute so the spice blooms into the masala.

---

### 7. Finish
- Add paneer, onion, and peppers back to the pan.
- Toss gently on medium for 2 minutes.
- Finish with garam masala and kasoori methi.
- Rest 3-5 minutes before serving.

---

## Notes
- Blanching, peeling, and blending the tomato avoids tomato skin in the finished masala.
- Ghee works well for searing both the paneer and the onion/capsicum; mustard oil is used for building the masala.
- Do not cook the paneer hard after adding it back or it will toughen.
- Keep the masala semi-dry and coating-style.

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Roti
- Paratha
- Kulcha

---

## Variants
- For a slightly saucier version, add 2-3 tbsp water after the kadhai spice and simmer briefly before adding paneer back.

---

## Personal Notes
- First tested with 225 g paneer.
- Onion and peppers were slightly raw after 3 minutes on high; 4 minutes works better.
- Preferred fat split: 1 tbsp ghee for searing paneer and onion/capsicum, then 1 tbsp mustard oil for the masala.
- Tomato was blanched, peeled, and blended because tomato skin in the finished dish is undesirable.
