# Boondi Kadhi

## Summary
Simple North Indian-style boondi kadhi with a smooth yogurt-besan base and a light tadka.  
The kadhi should be tangy, flowing, and simmered long enough for the raw besan taste to disappear.

---

## Ingredients

### Kadhi
- [[yogurt|Yogurt / curd]] - 1 cup, preferably slightly sour
- Besan - 3 tbsp
- Water - 3 cups, plus more if needed
- Turmeric - 1/2 tsp
- Kashmiri chilli powder - 1/2 tsp, optional
- Salt - to taste
- Sugar - 1/2 tsp, optional, only to balance sourness
- Boondi - 3/4 cup

### Tadka
- Ghee or oil - 1 1/2 tbsp
- Cumin seeds - 1 tsp
- Mustard seeds - 1/2 tsp, optional
- Fenugreek seeds - 1/4 tsp
- Dry red chillies - 2
- Asafoetida - pinch
- Curry leaves - 8-10, optional
- Ginger - 1 tsp, finely chopped or grated
- Green chilli - 1, slit

### Finish
- Garam masala - small pinch, optional
- Coriander leaves - optional

---

## Method

### 1. Whisk kadhi base
- Whisk yogurt, besan, turmeric, chilli powder, salt, and 1 cup water until completely smooth.
- Add the remaining 2 cups water and whisk again.

---

### 2. Make tadka
- Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan on medium.
- Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds if using, fenugreek seeds, dry red chillies, and asafoetida.
- Let the seeds sizzle without letting the fenugreek darken too much.
- Add ginger, green chilli, and curry leaves if using.
- Cook for 30 seconds.

---

### 3. Simmer kadhi
- Lower the heat and pour in the yogurt-besan mixture while stirring.
- Bring slowly to a simmer on medium heat, stirring often so it does not split or catch.
- Once it starts bubbling, reduce to low and simmer 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
- Add water if it thickens too much.

**Cue:** kadhi should be smooth, lightly tangy, and thick but pourable.

---

### 4. Add boondi
- Add boondi in the last 5 minutes if you like it soft.
- Add boondi just before serving if you prefer some texture.
- Finish with a tiny pinch of garam masala if using.

---

## Notes
- The raw besan smell should disappear before serving.
- Keep the simmer gentle after the yogurt base goes in.
- Add sugar only if the yogurt is very sharp.
- Boondi should swell slightly but not dissolve.

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Plain rice
- Pickle
- Papad

---

## Variants
- Softer boondi: simmer boondi in the kadhi for the final 5 minutes.
- Textured boondi: add boondi directly to the serving bowl.
