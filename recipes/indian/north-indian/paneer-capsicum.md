# Paneer Capsicum

## Summary
Dry paneer sabzi designed to pair with kulcha or roti.  
Masala is minimal and acts as a coating, not a gravy.

---

## Ingredients

- [[paneer]] – 250 g, cubed
- Mini sweet peppers – 8–10, cut into chunks
- [[onion|Onion]] – 1 large, sliced
- Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tbsp
- Oil – 2½ tbsp
- Salt – to taste

### Spices
- Turmeric – ¼ tsp
- Kashmiri chilli powder – 1 tsp
- Coriander powder – 1½ tsp
- Cumin powder – ½ tsp
- Garam masala – ½ tsp
- Kasoori methi – 1 tsp, crushed

### Optional
- Tomato puree – 1 tbsp
- Black pepper – ¼ tsp
- Lemon juice or amchur – small pinch
- Butter – 1 tsp

---

## Method

### 1. Sear paneer
- Heat 1 tbsp oil
- Lightly brown paneer on 2 sides
- Remove

**Why:** improves structure for stuffing or scooping

---

### 2. Cook onion and peppers
- Add remaining oil
- Cook onion until just soft
- Add peppers, cook 1–2 min on high

**Cue:** peppers must retain crunch

---

### 3. Add masala
- Add ginger-garlic paste
- Add spices + splash water
- Cook until glossy

---

### 4. Bind
- Add minimal tomato puree (or skip)
- Cook until oil just separates

**Important:** stop before it turns saucy

---

### 5. Finish
- Add paneer
- Toss gently
- Add garam masala + kasoori methi
- Optional butter
- Cook 2–3 min only

---

### 6. Balance
- Taste
- If slightly sweet → add pepper or acid

---

## Notes

### Texture
- Must be dry, coating-style masala
- No visible gravy

### Key control
- Capsicum timing defines dish quality

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Kulcha (ideal)
- Roti
