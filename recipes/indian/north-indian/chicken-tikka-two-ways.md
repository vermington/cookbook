# Chicken Tikka Two Ways

## Summary
Boneless chicken thigh tikka split into two marinades: classic red tikka and hariyali tikka.  
Designed for the AEG oven on fan + grill mode, with a short final top-rack char.

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken
- [[chicken|Boneless skinless chicken thigh fillets]] - 1 kg

### Red tikka marinade
- Thick [[yogurt]] or hung curd - 4 tbsp
- Mustard oil or neutral oil - 1 tbsp
- [[ginger|Ginger]]-[[garlic]] paste - 1 tbsp
- Kashmiri chilli powder - 1 1/2 tsp
- Hot chilli powder - 1/2 tsp, optional
- Coriander powder - 1 tsp
- Cumin powder - 1/2 tsp
- Garam masala - 1/2 tsp
- Turmeric - 1/4 tsp
- Kasoori methi - 1 tsp, crushed
- Lemon juice - 1 tbsp
- Salt - 1 tsp

### Hariyali paste
- Coriander leaves and tender stems - 1 packed cup
- Mint leaves - 1/2 packed cup
- Green chillies - 2-3
- Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tbsp
- Lemon juice - 1 tbsp
- Thick yogurt - 1-2 tbsp, only as needed to blend

### Hariyali marinade
- Thick yogurt or hung curd - 3 tbsp
- Mustard oil or neutral oil - 1 tbsp
- Coriander powder - 1 tsp
- Cumin powder - 1/2 tsp
- Garam masala - 1/2 tsp
- Turmeric - 1/4 tsp, optional
- Chaat masala - 1/2 tsp, optional
- Salt - 1 tsp

### Finish
- Oil or ghee - for brushing
- Lemon wedges - to serve
- Chaat masala - to serve, optional

---

## Method

### 1. Cut chicken
- Trim only thick loose fat or ragged edges.
- Cut the thigh fillets into large tikka pieces, about 4-5 cm each.
- Most thigh fillets should give 3 pieces; very large fillets may give 4.
- Divide into two 500 g batches.

**Cue:** pieces should be large enough to stay juicy under the grill, not small curry pieces.

---

### 2. Make red tikka marinade
- Mix all red tikka marinade ingredients into a thick paste.
- Add one 500 g batch of chicken.
- Coat thoroughly, cover, and refrigerate overnight.

---

### 3. Make hariyali marinade
- Blend the hariyali paste ingredients until smooth and thick.
- Mix with the remaining hariyali marinade ingredients.
- Add the second 500 g batch of chicken.
- Coat thoroughly, cover, and refrigerate overnight.

**Cue:** both marinades should cling to the chicken, not pool at the bottom.

---

### 4. Prepare for grilling
- Remove chicken from the fridge 20-30 minutes before cooking.
- Thread onto skewers with small gaps between pieces.
- Line a tray and set the skewers over the tray so heat can circulate.
- Brush lightly with oil or ghee.

---

### 5. Grill
- Set the AEG oven to 220°C fan + grill mode.
- Place skewers on the second rack from the top.
- Grill for 10 minutes.
- Move to the top rack.
- Grill for 5 minutes more to char.

**Cue:** edges should be lightly charred, chicken should be opaque through the centre, and juices should run clear.

---

### 6. Rest and serve
- Rest 5 minutes before serving.
- Finish with lemon and optional chaat masala.

---

## Notes
- Boneless thigh fillets are the right cut here: juicy, grill-friendly, and more forgiving than breast.
- Do not cut the chicken too small; small boneless pieces dry quickly.
- If using a thermometer, target 74°C in the thickest piece.
- Keep red and hariyali batches separate for colour and flavour clarity.
- If the tray is crowded, grill in batches rather than packing the skewers tightly.

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Serve as a starter with onion-lemon salad.
- Works with green chutney, sliced onions, and lemon wedges.
- For a fuller meal, serve with roomali roti, naan, or a simple pulao.

---

## Variants
- Paneer-capsicum tikka: use 250 g paneer and 1 large capsicum with a thicker, less acidic version of either marinade. Grill separately for 8-10 minutes max.
- Hob finish: sear cooked pieces briefly on a hot tawa if the oven char is insufficient.

---

## Personal Notes
- AEG oven timing: 10 minutes on the second rack from the top, then 5 minutes on the top rack for charring, both at 220°C fan + grill mode.
