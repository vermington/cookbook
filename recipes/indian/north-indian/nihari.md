# Nihari

## Summary
A slow-cooked mutton stew where collagen breakdown creates a naturally thick, silky gravy.  
This version uses Shan masala as a base but follows correct technique for depth, texture, and finish.

---

## Ingredients

### Meat
- Goat/mutton (bone-in, curry cut; include shank if possible) – 1–1.2 kg

### Base
- Onions – 2 large, thinly sliced
- Ginger-garlic paste – 2 tbsp
- Shan Nihari masala – ½–¾ packet (adjust to taste)
- Ghee or oil – ½ cup
- Water – 6–7 cups (hot)

### Thickening
- Atta (whole wheat flour) – 2 tbsp
- Hot water – ¾ cup

### Garnish
- Fresh ginger – julienned
- Coriander – chopped
- Green chillies – slit
- Lemon wedges

---

## Method

### 1. Fry onions (critical for depth)
- Heat ghee/oil on medium.
- Fry sliced onions until **deep golden (not burnt)**.
- Remove ~50% and reserve for garnish.

**Cue:** onions should be sweet, nutty, not sharp.

---

### 2. Brown meat
- Add mutton to remaining onions.
- Cook 8–10 min until lightly browned.

**Why:** initial Maillard reactions improve flavour.

---

### 3. Build base
- Add ginger-garlic paste → cook 1–2 min until raw smell disappears.
- Add Shan masala → fry briefly (do not burn).

---

### 4. Transfer to slow cooker
- Add hot water.
- Stir well.

### Cook:
- LOW → 7–8 hours

**Target state:**
- Meat is **tender but still holds shape**
- Not yet shredding into fibres

---

### 5. Finish on hob (important)
Transfer to heavy pot.

#### Prepare slurry:
- Roast atta lightly (optional but improves flavour)
- Mix with hot water until smooth

#### Add to pot:
- Bring nihari to gentle simmer
- Add slurry gradually, stirring continuously

Simmer **15–20 minutes**

**Key cues:**
- Gravy becomes glossy
- Slight oil layer appears on top
- No raw flour taste

---

### 6. Rest
Turn off heat, cover, rest 20 minutes.

---

## Notes

### Texture control
- Undercook → tough meat
- Overcook → stringy fibres (you experienced this)
- Ideal: spoon-tender, intact chunks

### Why finish on hob
- Better control of thickening
- Avoids over-reduction in slow cooker

### Next-day improvement
- Flavour improves significantly after overnight rest

---

## Serving Suggestions
- With naan or khameeri roti
- Or plain rice
- Always serve with ginger, lemon, and coriander

---
