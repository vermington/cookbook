# Fried Rice

## Summary
Dry, high-heat Indo-Chinese fried rice where separation of grains and clean flavour are key.  
This version is balanced for home cooking without losing the canteen-style profile.

---

## Ingredients

- [[rice|Cooked rice]] – from 1 cup raw rice (completely cooled)
- Eggs – 2
- Frozen mixed veg – ¾ cup
- Frozen peas – ¼ cup
- [[garlic|Garlic]] – 4–5 cloves, finely chopped
- Green chilli – 1, finely chopped (optional)
- Oil – 2 tbsp (neutral)
- Soy sauce – 1½ tsp
- Vinegar – 1 tsp
- MSG – ¼ tsp
- Salt – to taste
- Black pepper – ½ tsp

---

## Method

### 1. Prepare rice
- Break up rice gently with fingers.
- No clumps should remain.

**Cue:** grains must be separate before hitting the pan.

---

### 2. Scramble eggs
- Heat 1 tsp oil on high.
- Add eggs + pinch salt.
- Scramble lightly until just set.

Remove immediately.

**Why:** overcooked eggs become rubbery later.

---

### 3. Stir-fry aromatics
- Add remaining oil.
- Add garlic (and chilli if using).
- Fry 10–15 seconds only.

**Cue:** fragrant, not browned.

---

### 4. Cook vegetables
- Add frozen veg directly.
- Cook on high heat 2–3 minutes.

**Goal:**
- evaporate moisture
- keep slight bite

---

### 5. Season base
- Add soy sauce + vinegar
- Add MSG + pepper + light salt

Toss quickly.

**Why:** MSG disperses best in hot oil phase before rice.

---

### 6. Add rice
- Add rice and toss continuously on high heat.
- Let sit briefly between tosses (10–15 sec) for slight drying.

---

### 7. Finish
- Add eggs back
- Toss 1–2 minutes until hot and evenly mixed

---

## Notes

### Texture
- Correct result is:
  - dry
  - non-sticky
  - lightly glossy

### Common mistakes
- Low heat → soggy rice
- Too much soy → wet, dark rice
- Fresh rice → clumping

---

## Serving Suggestions
- Serve immediately
- Optional: chilli vinegar or chilli sauce
